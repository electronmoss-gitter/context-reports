# Australian and International Standards for Earthing Systems
# Reference Document for AI Report Generation

## AS/NZS 3000:2018 - Electrical Installations (Wiring Rules)

### Section 5.6 - Earthing Arrangements

**Clause 5.6.1.1 - Main Earthing Conductor**
The main earthing conductor shall be:
- Copper: minimum 4 mm² for final sub-circuits, 6 mm² for distribution circuits
- Aluminum: minimum 16 mm² 
- For installations with multiple incoming supplies > 35mm²: minimum 16 mm² copper or 35 mm² aluminum

**Clause 5.6.2.1 - Size of Main Earthing Conductor**
Minimum size from Table 5.1:
- Supply ≤ 100A: 4mm² copper / 16mm² aluminum
- Supply 100-200A: 6mm² copper / 16mm² aluminum  
- Supply > 200A: 16mm² copper / 35mm² aluminum

**Clause 5.6.2.2 - Earthing Conductor Connections**
All connections shall be:
- Mechanically and electrically sound
- Protected against corrosion
- Accessible for inspection (except buried connections)
- Exothermic welding, compression fittings, or bolted connections

**Clause 5.7 - Bonding**
All extraneous-conductive-parts shall be bonded to the main earthing terminal:
- Metal water pipes
- Metal gas pipes
- Structural steelwork
- Lightning protection systems
- Other services

### Section 8.3 - Protection Against Electric Shock

**Clause 8.3.2 - Earthed Equipotential Bonding and Automatic Disconnection**
Protection by automatic disconnection requires:
- All exposed-conductive-parts connected to earthing system
- Protective device to disconnect within specified time
- Earth fault loop impedance sufficiently low

## AS 2067:2016 - Substations and High Voltage Installations

### Section 4.3 - Earthing System Design

**Clause 4.3.1 - General Requirements**
The earthing system shall:
- Provide safe dissipation of fault currents
- Maintain touch and step potentials within safe limits
- Provide effective earth reference for equipment
- Maintain integrity under all operating conditions

**Clause 4.3.2 - Earth Resistance**
Maximum earth resistance values:
- For substations ≤ 66kV: typically ≤ 5 Ω
- For substations > 66kV: typically ≤ 1 Ω
- Lower values may be required based on fault current and protection requirements

**Clause 4.3.3 - Earth Grid Construction**
Earth grids shall:
- Use corrosion-resistant conductors (copper or copper-bonded steel)
- Be buried at adequate depth (typically 0.5-0.8m)
- Include multiple earth electrodes
- Cover area of major equipment

**Clause 4.4 - Touch and Step Potentials**
Touch and step potentials shall not exceed limits calculated per IEEE 80 or equivalent:
- Consider fault current magnitude
- Consider fault duration
- Consider soil resistivity
- Consider surface material resistivity

**Clause 4.5 - Earthing Conductors**

**Clause 4.5.1 - Minimum Sizes**
Buried earthing conductors:
- Copper: minimum 50 mm² for grids
- Copper-clad steel: minimum 70 mm²
- Above-ground bonds: minimum 16 mm² copper

**Clause 4.5.2 - Conductor Sizing for Fault Current**
Conductors shall withstand maximum fault current without damage:
- Calculate using adiabatic equation (AS/NZS 3008.1.1)
- Consider fault duration
- Consider heat dissipation to soil

**Clause 4.5.3 - Connections**
All buried connections:
- Exothermic welding (Cadweld or equivalent)
- Compression fittings (for special applications)
- Minimum two connections for reliability

### Section 5.2 - Equipment Earthing

**Clause 5.2.1 - Equipment Bonding**
All metallic non-current-carrying parts shall be bonded:
- Transformer tanks and enclosures
- Switchgear structures and panels
- Cable metallic sheaths and armour
- Metallic fences and gates
- Metallic building structures

## IEEE 80-2013 - Guide for Safety in AC Substation Grounding

### Section 8 - Tolerable Touch and Step Voltages

**Equation 8.1 - Tolerable Touch Voltage (50-70kg person)**
E_touch = (1000 + 1.5 × ρ_s × C_s) × (0.116 / √t_s)

Where:
- ρ_s = surface layer resistivity (Ω·m)
- C_s = surface layer derating factor
- t_s = duration of shock current (seconds)
- 0.116 = empirical constant for 50-70kg person

**Equation 8.2 - Tolerable Step Voltage**
E_step = (1000 + 6 × ρ_s × C_s) × (0.116 / √t_s)

**Table 8.1 - Surface Layer Derating Factor**
C_s = 1 - (0.09 × (1 - ρ/ρ_s)) / (2h_s + 0.09)

Where:
- ρ = earth resistivity (Ω·m)  
- ρ_s = surface layer resistivity (Ω·m)
- h_s = thickness of surface layer (m)

### Section 14 - Resistance of Ground System

**Equation 14.7 - Schwarz's Equation (Grid Resistance)**
R_g = ρ / (4√A) × [1 + 1/(1 + h√(A/20))]

Where:
- ρ = soil resistivity (Ω·m)
- A = grid area (m²)
- h = depth of grid burial (m)

**Equation 14.15 - Grid with Ground Rods**
R_g = R_g(grid) || R_r(rods)
Considers parallel resistance of grid and vertical rods

### Section 15 - Design Procedures

**15.1 - Basic Design Steps**
1. Conduct field survey and soil resistivity measurements
2. Determine maximum grid current
3. Calculate tolerable touch and step voltages
4. Design preliminary grid
5. Calculate actual touch and step voltages
6. Iterate design if necessary

**15.8 - Mesh Voltage (Touch Potential)**
E_mesh = (ρ × K_m × K_i × I_f) / L_m

Where:
- K_m = geometric spacing factor
- K_i = irregularity factor  
- I_f = maximum fault current (A)
- L_m = effective buried conductor length (m)

**15.9 - Step Voltage**
E_step = (ρ × K_s × K_i × I_f) / L_s

Where:
- K_s = geometric spacing factor for step voltage
- L_s = effective buried conductor length for step (m)

## AS/NZS 3008.1.1:2017 - Conductor Sizing for Fault Currents

### Section 3.4 - Short Circuit Temperature Rise

**Equation 3.1 - Adiabatic Equation**
A = I × √[t / (K × ln((T_CAP + α) / (T_max + α)))]

Where:
- A = minimum conductor cross-sectional area (mm²)
- I = fault current (A)
- t = fault duration (seconds)
- K = material constant (226 for copper, 148 for aluminum)
- T_CAP = melting point (°C) - 1083°C for copper
- α = temperature coefficient (234.5 for copper, 228 for aluminum)
- T_max = maximum allowable temperature (°C)

**Table 3.1 - Material Constants**
Copper:
- K = 226
- α = 234.5
- T_CAP = 1083°C

Aluminum:  
- K = 148
- α = 228
- T_CAP = 657°C

**Clause 3.4.2 - Maximum Temperatures**
For bare buried conductors:
- Copper: typically 200-300°C (conservative)
- Copper in air: 250-450°C (depending on joint type)

## AS/NZS 5033:2014 - Installation of Photovoltaic (PV) Arrays

### Section 4.3 - Earthing

**Clause 4.3.1 - General**
PV arrays and associated equipment shall be earthed in accordance with AS/NZS 3000.

**Clause 4.3.2 - Array Structure Earthing**
Metallic support structures shall:
- Be bonded together electrically
- Be connected to main earthing system
- Use corrosion-resistant connections
- Minimum conductor: 16 mm² copper or 35 mm² aluminum

**Clause 4.3.3 - DC Earthing**
For systems with negative terminal earthed:
- Negative conductor bonded at one point only
- Connection as close to inverter as practical
- Earth fault detection required

**Clause 4.3.4 - Lightning Protection**
Array structures inherently provide lightning protection:
- Integrate with earthing system
- Provide surge protection devices (SPD)
- Bond all metallic components

## Common Earthing Design Requirements by Voltage Class

### Low Voltage (< 1kV)
- Target earth resistance: < 1 Ω (TN systems)
- Minimum conductor: 16 mm² copper for main earthing
- Standards: AS/NZS 3000 primary reference

### High Voltage (1kV - 66kV)
- Target earth resistance: typically < 5 Ω (1-2 Ω preferred)
- Minimum grid conductor: 50-70 mm² copper
- Touch/step analysis required
- Standards: AS 2067, IEEE 80

### Extra High Voltage (> 66kV)
- Target earth resistance: typically < 1-2 Ω
- Minimum grid conductor: 95-120 mm² copper
- Detailed touch/step analysis mandatory
- Surface treatment often required
- Standards: AS 2067, IEEE 80

## Typical Design Values (Australian Conditions)

### Soil Resistivity Ranges
- Very low: < 100 Ω·m (wet clay, coastal areas)
- Low: 100-300 Ω·m (moist soil, loam)
- Medium: 300-500 Ω·m (sandy soil, typical conditions)
- High: 500-1000 Ω·m (dry sandy soil, weathered rock)
- Very high: > 1000 Ω·m (rock, very dry conditions)

### Surface Layer Materials (for touch/step calculation)
- Crushed rock (20mm): 2,000-3,500 Ω·m
- Gravel (40mm): 3,000-5,000 Ω·m  
- Asphalt: 10,000-30,000 Ω·m
- Concrete: 1,000-10,000 Ω·m (varies with moisture)
- Grass/vegetation: 200-1,000 Ω·m (moisture dependent)

### Typical Fault Currents (Australian Networks)
- 415V LV: 10-50 kA
- 11kV: 8-20 kA
- 22kV: 10-25 kA
- 33kV: 15-30 kA
- 66kV: 20-40 kA
- 132kV: 25-50 kA

### Typical Protection Operating Times
- LV circuit breakers: 0.02-0.2 seconds
- HV instantaneous: 0.1-0.3 seconds
- HV time delayed: 0.3-1.0 seconds
- Backup protection: 1.0-3.0 seconds
